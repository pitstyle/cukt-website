Cyborg's Sex Manual

Educational CD - Room, 1999,
Concept, screenwriter, text: Piotr Wyrzykowski, 3D Animation: Rafał Ewertowski, programming: Adam van Der Haagen, voice: Haleh Abghari, audio design: Jarogniew Milewski, art director: Piotr Wyrzykowski, curator: Ewa Gorządek. 
Cyborg's Sex Manual is a CD-ROM-based interactive artwork by Piotr Wyrzykowski, presented as an installation with a 3x4m screen displaying interactive 3D graphics. It functions as a speculative manual from the near future, designed to introduce cyborgs—hybrid beings of human and machine—to the complexities of emotions, love, and intimacy. Rooted in research conducted by CUKT (Technical Culture Central Office) between 1995 and 1997, the work assumes the imminent or existing presence of such entities.
Blurring the boundaries between reality and fantasy, the piece explores sensitive topics such as love, responsibility, intimacy, identity, bodily limits, privacy, and human connection. By situating the discussion in a futuristic setting, Wyrzykowski creates a conceptual distance, allowing for a nuanced reflection on these themes. The visual component is accompanied by a hypertext titled O rozwiniętym ciele kultury technicznej (On the Developed Body of Technical Culture), which recontextualizes the erotic and sexual discourse, assigning it a new interpretative framework.
The project was conceived not only as an art installation but also as a commercially available CD-ROM, with plans for an advertising campaign spanning magazines, billboards, and the internet.

2.11 Simulation provides certitude.
2.12 Reason lasts for a moment.

2.14 Your body likes being used.

3.7 Identification is before language.
3.8 There is no such thing as a true objective meaning of a notion.
3.9 Every notion may be "read" by simulation in a unique way.
3.10 The meaning of a notion is defined by simulation.
3.11 Notions are being constantly redefined.
3.12 Your body passes from one body form to another.
3.13 Your touch transforms another one into the body.
3.14 The need for form change follows from your body.
4. Activity and Limitations4.1 Your body is doomed to develop.
4.2 Limits exist only within your program.
4.3 Your identification transgresses your program limits.
4.4 A simulation is a habitat for your activity.
4.5 Keep being constantly active.
4.6 Activity is what defines you.
4.7 Your "I" program is built of language.
4.8 There are zillions of versions of yourself.
4.9 Openness is acceptance of the variety of simulation.
4.10 Simulation verifies notions.
4.11 Openness is the main aim of simulation.
4.12 You are limited by the program.
4.13 Your body is not a thing but it is you yourself.
4.14 Your body is active.
5. The Open Body5.1 Your body needs space.
5.2 Your body is defined by limits.
5.3 Your identification opens the body up.
5.4 Your identification unites you with the body of simulation.
5.5 Your active identification unites you with your partner’s body.
5.6 The more the body is your body, the less it is present.
5.7 Your form is emptiness, emptiness is your form.
5.8 There is no body, there is no nose, there are no lips.
5.9 The existence of a perfect open body determines the limits of development.
5.10 The perfect body form annihilates notions.
5.11 There isn't and will not be a perfect open body form.
5.12 For man, the unlimited body is the idea of God.
5.13 The perfect body is a body which undergoes mutation.
5.14 You are undoubtedly attached to your body.
6. Simulation and the Future6.1 The simulation space is your body.
6.2 Your body is not a thing but it is you yourself.
6.3 By changing the body of information, you change simulation.
6.4 Information not only sustains simulation but also establishes its new course.
6.5 Body shapes body.
6.6 You can lose yourself in another body.
6.7 Don't be afraid to disappear.
6.8 For you, development means total dissolution of body.
6.9 Development means the future.
6.10 Simulation projects itself into body’s future.
6.11 Your activity makes simulation endless.
6.12 Every simulation is open to a sequel.
6.13 The changes in the space size of simulation is your body functioning.
6.14 Simulation strives to annihilate your body.
