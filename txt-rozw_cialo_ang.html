The developed Body of Technical Culture

The Developed Body Reference Guide. Handbook for Cyborgs.
                                                                                                                Cyborg in Your Perimeter



0.0 The limits of your language are the limits of your world.

L. Wittgenstein

0.1 Language is body.

0'.0' Does work require a body?

0.10 What isn't a body?

0'.1' Work is a process of organizing the body.

8'.0' Changes of simulation influence methods of organizing the body in order to make it more open.

0'.0' What is the total dissolution of a body?




0.2 The body is defined by limits.

1.2 Limits exist only within the program.

8.2 The body is the world within the limits of language.

0'.2' What is opposed to the body is opposed to language.




0.3 A body provokes notions.

1.3 A body allows ambiguity.

8.3 Ambiguity is a guarantee of openness.

0'.3' Ambiguity is compatibility of element with element.

8'.3' Every sign is related to another sign and achieves its full expression only in the context of a sign group, therefore its meaning is unclear.

U. Eco

0.4 Body provocation is accomplished in language.

8.4 Is language irrevocably assigned to the body?

1.4 Knowledge of the body determines language.

0'.4' Language is the essential basis of simulation. To read a message use language. Language confirms or refutes language.




0.5 There exists a bodily relationship between language and the world.

1'.5' The world is a limitless notion.

The world is a simulation-defined body.

1.5 When language defines the world, this action is secondary.

The world is a limitary body.

The world is the set of all bodies and simulations.

0'.5' One body defines another.

8.5 Language is speculation.



                                                                                            Cyborg Development. The Changes in You.




1.0 To what extent do you feel your body?

1.10 You perceive it through yourself.

1.1 You contact other bodies through the form of the body.

1'.1' You define the body from inside.

1'.0' "I" is the first body you organize.

1'.2' Your "I" is your program.




1.9 You exist through your organism.

1'.10' "I" is language and something else.

1'.9' Intuition has no body.

0.9 Do you exist?

8.9 Ultimately, existence is verified internally.

0'.9' Consciousness of existence is independent of external stimuli.

0'.10' There are no external stimuli.

8'.10' Life is simulation.




0.8 How little of you can exist?

0'.8' You can observe two facts, something extremely big and something extremely small.

0'.7' Within the spheres of the extremely big and small the body ceases to exist.

1'.8' You are undoubtedly attached to the body.

1.8 How does your "I" exist?

1.7 "I" constitutes itself in unison with world simulation.

1'.7' If the world exists, so does your "I".

1'.6' System provides certitude (not only of existence).




0.7 To feel is to process a piece of information.

0.6 The more complicated the cognitive process, the less simulation is used, the more it is renewed and open to new "readings". U. Eco.

8.7 The body is able to accept information without being conscious of the fact.




                                                                                                    On Construction without Secrets




2.0 Your body is the information you identify with.

2.10 Information is not only a message but also the spearhead of being.
W.Sedlak

2'.0' You believe in information.

2.2 Information as a transformation agent.

2'.1' Information as the cement of systems.

2'.2' Information as a guarantee of activity.







2.9 Information not only sustains simulation but also establishes its new course.

2'.9' By changing the body of information we change simulation.

2'.10' If change lies in the nature of the body then information has its own program.




1'.10' Your program awaits interpretation.

The program, like the body, is ambiguous.

2.8. Identification removes limits.

2.7 Identification - a necessary cement of systems.

2'.7' You believe in systems, not in individual laws.

3'.7' A system is a habitat for activity.

2'.8' You can't treat your emblem and its tonal representation as yourself if you identify with them.




2.6 Identification transforms the body.

2'.6' Identification is a sign of accord, admission, acceptance.

1.6 The deeper your identification the richer your body.




2.5 How deep can identification be?

2'.5' Identification opens the body up.

3'.6' Identification unites you with the body of simulation.

3'.5' Openness is acceptance of the variety of simulation.

3.6 The egotism of man enclosed by the body of his Maker has nothing to do with openness.

2.4 Identification concerns the essence of the awareness of oneself, (mine).

2'.4' "Mine" is limited by the program.

2'.2' Society dictates the nature of simulation.

1'.3' The program of individuals controls society.

3.4 Identification is the strength of simulation.

2.2 The individual identifies with society.

1'.4' Body shapes body.




2.3 Identification defines the body's assets.

2'.3' Identification, or the verification of belonging, the unique subjectivity of the gesture of choosing.

3'.3' Identification is before language.



3.0 The simulation space is your body.

3.1 The change of the simulation space size is a function of the body.

3'.0' The body needs space.

2.1 All the social phenomena - program and simulation - originate in your attitude towards your body.




3.10 A graph of the variable size of simulation space is a curve of the embodiment of thought.

3'.10' The need for form change follows from the body.

3.9 The body is active.

3'.9' The perfect body is a body which undergoes mutation.




3.2 There isn't and won't be a uniquely proper body.

The existence of a uniquely proper body would determine the limits of development.

For man, the uniquely proper body is the idea of god.

3'.1' God as a neverending development process is not a mystery.

3'.2' God, like everything, is information to be processed.




4.0 What is simulation?

4'.0' Simulation is the starting point for another simulation.

4.10 Every simulation is open to a sequel.

4.9 Simulation is a more or less controlled hallucination.

4'.10' An uncontrolled hallucination follows from the body attempting to

free itself from the simulation program.

4'.9' Simulation strives to annihilate the body.




4.1 Simulation verifies notions.

4'.1' Simulations are built by information.

4.2 An notion is a single word, simulation is an endless sentence.

4.8 There is no such thing as a true objective meaning of a notion.

4.9 Every notion may be "read" by everybody in their unique way.

4'.2' The meaning of notions is defined by simulation.




5.0 How does simulation exist in reality? back

5'.0' Simulation exists through the body of the simulation participant.

5'.1' Openness - the main problem of simulation.

5.1 What is reality?

5.2 Simulation is reality or it endows reality with new bodies and notions.

5'.2' Reality is a fact in contrast to simulation, which becomes a fact.




6.2 What is reality like?

5'.3' Reality is omnipresent.

5.3 What is not reality?

6.3 Simulation becomes reality.

6.4 Reality simply is, simulation projects itself into being.




5.10 Simulation and reality mutually confirm their existence.

5.9 Simulation is a part of reality, only a little more real.

5.8 Reality has a simulation complex.

6.0 Transgressing simulation is extending the limits of the body.

6.1 The section of reality taken to be one's own body is identical with the range of simulation.




6.10 Simulation is a bodily function.

6'.10' The body is active.

6'.9' The body likes being used.

7'.9' A live body - an active body.

6.9 Simulation is for the living.




6'.0' The body is doomed to develop, whatever development means.

6'.1' One body is a whole species.

6'.2' "I" is what has been, what is, and what will be.

6'.3' Development means the future.




7.0 The body is part of your simulation

7'.0' The body is able to sustain a part of the aggression, hate, fear and adoration of the modern times.

7.3 Simulation takes over the body's great need for activity.

7.2 Simulation is for the body a safer form of hallucination.

7'.1' We are where we belong or not.




7.1 "I" am responsible for simulation.

8.0 The purpose of existence of simulation and the body - continuum. back

8.10 Notions are being constantly redefined.

7.10 Simulation has no reason.

7'.10' Reason lasts a moment.




8.1 Body passes from body to body changing the perception of simulation.

8'.1' ...form is emptiness, emptiness is form. No body, no nose, no mouth...

A Buddhist mantra.

7'.2' If you are here then you know why you are here.



Peter Style, Gyspy Hill, 1995.
