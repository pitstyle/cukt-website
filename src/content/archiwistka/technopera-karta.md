# PROJECT CARD: TECHNOPERA (1996–1997)

## BASIC DATA
- **Date:** 1996–1997 (premiery: Zielona Góra 1996, Warszawa CSW Ujazdowski 1996, Dessau 1997, Wrocław WRO 1997, Banff Kanada 1997, Budapeszt)
- **Location:** Galeria WRO (Wrocław, Polska), CSW Zamek Ujazdowski (Warszawa, Polska), Dessau (Niemcy), Banff Centre (Kanada), Budapeszt (Węgry)
- **Authors:** Piotr Wyrzykowski (libretto), Artur Kozdrowski (libretto)
- **Medium:** Multimedia performance / video installation / audio-visual libretto
- **Duration:** Variable (video clips 1:17–28:55, total libretto 217 video files)

## DESCRIPTION
Technopera was a multimedia performance and video installation created by CUKT collective. The work combined live performance with video projections, electronic music, and an audio-visual "libretto" consisting of 217 video files containing text and AV samples. Performances featured live performers speaking into microphones alongside projected imagery, creating a hybrid experience blending historical and technological elements. One documented performance in Wrocław (1997) showed a bearded performer (Piotr Wyrzykowski) in a suit, a woman in 18th/19th century costume, and video projections of the same performer with different head coverings (turban/headphones). The libretto included scenes depicting medical/surgical interventions with text such as "It looks like he's just control" and "THERE IS NO BABY," exploring themes of bodily control, cybernetic transformation, and anti-natalist concepts.
