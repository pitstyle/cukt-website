PROJECT CARD: 120h Mega Techno Obecności

## BASIC DATA
- **Date:** December 2-13, 1994 (7 days active: Dec 2-8; 5 days safety buffer: Dec 9-13)
- **Location:** Pawilon 'Rondo', ul. Przemysłowa 9, Konin, Poland; 'Pracownia Chwilowa' (basement)
- **Authors:** Piotr Wyrzykowski, Robert Jurkowski
- **Medium:** Socio-aesthetic experiment / performance intervention
- **Duration:** 7 days of activity

## DESCRIPTION
The project transformed a commercial gallery space into a techno club operating from 18:00-04:00, reversing the conventional gallery hours (10:00-18:00). The gallery 'Pracownia Chwilowa' in the 'Rondo' shopping center basement was converted into a nightclub without prior notice to local residents.

Central installations included:
- A large red cylindrical column with vertical grooves (by Robert Jurkowski), painted with phosphorescent orange paint
- The column base held a vessel with honey and amphetamine, illuminated from below, creating light and scent effects
- Walls displayed phosphorescent texts and philosophical statements (Wittgenstein, Piotrowski's 'hypertext')
- A disco ball hung from the ceiling
- Audio equipment: mixers, cassette decks, microphones

The project ended after 7 days due to safety concerns when local criminal groups ('półświadkowie Konina' - Konin's semi-witnesses/gangsters) attempted to take control of the space, creating a dangerous rivalry between two criminal groups. The remaining 5 days served as a 'safety buffer' for dissolution.

Upon departure, unknown perpetrators burned down the gallery doors, leaving a copy of the closure letter dated December 9. This act has been interpreted as a ritual offering of the space.
